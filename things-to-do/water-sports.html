Water Sports & Sea Activities

The clear, calm waters of the Costa Brava make it one of the best spots in the Mediterranean for water sports of all kinds. Tamariu's sheltered cove is a perfect base — the sea here is remarkably clear, and the rocky headlands just offshore are rich with marine life.

Paddle Boarding

Stand-up paddleboarding (SUP) is wonderful in Tamariu's calm bay, especially in the early morning before any breeze picks up. Board hire is available on the beach in summer. Paddling out along the cliffs at sunrise is unforgettable.

Boat Hire

Small motorboats can be hired from the beach in Tamariu during summer, allowing you to explore the hidden coves and sea caves of the coastline north and south — many of which are inaccessible by foot. No licence required for smaller craft under 5HP. Ask locally on arrival for current hire availability.

Snorkelling

The rocky reefs directly off Tamariu beach are excellent for snorkelling, with good visibility and a variety of fish, octopus and sea urchins. The Posidonia seagrass meadows just offshore are a protected habitat and a sign of the sea's exceptional water quality. Bring your own mask and fins or hire from beach kiosks in summer.

Scuba Diving

The Costa Brava is one of the best diving destinations in the Mediterranean, with dramatic underwater topography, excellent visibility and abundant marine life. We particularly recommend Stollis Dive Base, an excellent and well-regarded dive centre operating in the area.

Stollis Dive Base

Professional dive centre offering courses, guided dives and equipment hire on the Costa Brava. Suitable for all levels from beginners to advanced divers.

Kayaking

Sea kayaking along this stretch of coastline is exceptional — guided excursions run from Tamariu southward along the cliffs, exploring sea caves, snorkelling spots and remote coves. Kayaking Costa Brava offers a superb half-day guided excursion departing from Tamariu.

Kayaking Costa Brava — Tamariu South Half-Day

A guided sea kayak excursion from Tamariu heading south along the dramatic coastline. Includes snorkelling stops and exploration of hidden sea caves. Suitable for all abilities.

→

Tips for the Sea

  • The sea in Tamariu is calm but check conditions before heading out — the Tramuntana wind can arrive quickly
  • Water shoes are useful on the rocky entry points around the bay
  • Sun protection is essential — the Mediterranean sun is strong even with a sea breeze
  • The beach has a lifeguard in summer; flags indicate sea conditions
  • Book kayak and dive excursions in advance in July and August
