Cycling the Costa Brava

The Costa Brava and surrounding Empordà plain make for exceptional cycling territory. Whether you're after gentle coastal rollers or challenging climbs into the Gavarres hills, there's a route for every rider — and the roads are quiet, well-surfaced and stunningly scenic.

Popular Routes from Tamariu

🚴 The Coastal Circuit (35 km | Easy–Moderate)

A classic loop linking Tamariu, Llafranc, Calella de Palafrugell and Palafrugell before returning via quiet inland lanes. Minimal climbing with beautiful sea views throughout. Perfect for a morning spin followed by a dip at the beach.

🚴 Gavarres Hills Loop (65 km | Challenging)

Head inland from Palafrugell into the Gavarres massif — a range of low wooded hills offering some excellent climbs and fast descents. The roads are almost entirely traffic-free and the views back towards the coast are spectacular.

🚴 Empordà Plain Classic (80 km | Moderate)

A longer ride out across the flat agricultural plain of the Baix Empordà, passing through medieval villages including Peratallada, Pals and Torroella de Montgrí before the return along quiet country lanes. Ideal in spring when the landscape is lush and green.

🚴 Begur–Palamós–Tamariu Triangle (50 km | Moderate)

A varied circuit taking in the hilltop castle town of Begur, the working port of Palamós and the quieter coves in between. Some short sharp climbs but rewarded with long smooth descents.

🏆 Gran Fondo Events

The Costa Brava hosts several gran fondo events throughout the spring and summer. The Triatló Costa Brava and various cyclosportive events organised through local cycling clubs attract riders from across Europe. The famously beautiful L'Étape du Tour route through the Pyrenees is within a day's drive for more ambitious riders.
