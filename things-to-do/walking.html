Walking in the Area

The coastline and hinterland around Tamariu offer superb walking for all abilities — from gentle strolls between coves to longer hikes through the cork oak forests of the Gavarres. The famous GR-92 coastal path passes right through the village.

Featured Walks

Walk to the Beach — 3 km Circular from Tamariu Chalet

A gentle 3 km loop from the chalet down to Aigua Xelida beach and back — about 90 minutes round trip.

Speed

Walk 1 — Tamariu Chalet to Aigua Xelida Coastal Circuit

A classic coastal walk along the costal path. Approximately 3.2km one way. Easy–moderate.

→

Walk 2 — Tamariu to Ses Falugues

A circular climb to the summit of Ses Falugues with an exceptional viewpoint, pausing at the Aigua Xelida lookout before dropping down past Sa Roncadora and its small coves — a final stretch with plenty of steps. 8 km, loop. Moderate.

→

Walk 3 — Tamariu to Aiguafreda & Sa Riera

Head north along the coast from Tamariu towards the unspoiled coves of Aiguafreda and Sa Riera. About km. Moderate.

→

Walk 4 — Circular Palafrugell: Tamariu, Far Sant Sebastià, Llafranc & Calella

A scenic loop from Palafrugell down to Tamariu, along the coast to the Sant Sebastià lighthouse, then on through Llafranc and Calella before returning inland to Palafrugell. 21.5 km, loop. Difficult.

→

Walk 5 — Tamariu to El Far de Sant Sebastià

A loop out to the Sant Sebastià lighthouse on interior trails, returning to Tamariu along the scenic coastal path (camí de ronda). 9 km, loop. Easy.

→

Walking Tips

  • The coastal path can be rocky and uneven — proper walking shoes are recommended
  • Carry water — there are few water sources on the coastal path
  • Start early in summer to avoid the heat — paths are shaded in the Gavarres but fully exposed on the coast
  • The GR-92 is marked with red and white paint blazes on rocks and trees
  • Several coves along the way are swimmable — pack a towel and snorkel
