Local Markets

The markets of the Baix Empordà are a highlight of any stay — bustling, colourful and packed with local produce, artisan crafts and the atmosphere of authentic Catalan life.

Weekly Markets

🛒 Palafrugell Market — Wednesday & Saturday mornings

The most convenient market for guests at Tamariu Chalet — just 4 km away. Palafrugell's twice-weekly market fills the town square with stalls of fresh fruit and vegetables, olives, charcuterie, cheese, flowers and household goods. The Saturday market is larger and particularly lively.

🛒 Pals Market — Tuesday mornings

A charming market in the medieval village of Pals, selling local produce and artisan goods. The village itself — with its 15th-century Gothic tower and cobbled lanes — is worth a visit in its own right.

🛒 Palamós Market — Tuesday & Friday mornings

A larger town market in the working port of Palamós, strong on fish and seafood (naturally), fresh produce and clothing. The fish auction at the harbour is worth witnessing if you're up early.

🛒 Begur Market — Wednesday mornings

A popular market beneath the castle walls of Begur. Good mix of local produce, artisan crafts and antiques. The town's cafés are an excellent spot for breakfast beforehand.

Artisan & Craft Markets

Throughout the summer, evening craft markets and artisan fairs pop up in coastal villages along the coast — Calella de Palafrugell and Llafranc both host regular summer evening markets. Ask us for the current schedule when you arrive.

Tips for Market Shopping

  • Bring cash — not all stalls accept cards
  • Go early for the best selection of fresh produce
  • Bring a bag or basket — plastic bags are rarely provided
  • Try the local honey, olive oil, wine and artisan cheeses — all exceptional
  • Markets typically run until 13:00–14:00
