Life on the Costa Brava

Tamariu is one of the Costa Brava's best-kept secrets — a small, unhurried fishing village nestled in a sheltered cove where pine trees meet clear turquoise water. Unlike its livelier neighbours, Tamariu has resisted mass tourism, preserving the charm of whitewashed houses, small fishing boats and the gentle rhythm of Mediterranean life.

The village sits within the municipality of Palafrugell, in the heart of the Costa Brava — literally the "Wild Coast" — a stretch of rugged Catalan coastline that unfolds between the Pyrenees and Barcelona. Here, dramatic limestone cliffs plunge into the sea, carving out hidden coves and crystalline inlets accessible only by boat or narrow footpath.

Beyond the beach, the surrounding hinterland rewards exploration. Medieval hilltop towns such as Pals, Peratallada and Begur rise from golden plains dotted with vineyards and olive groves. The city of Girona — with its Roman walls, cathedral and famous Jewish Quarter — is just 40 minutes by car, making a superb day trip.

The area is a cyclist's paradise: rolling country roads, the famous L'Étape du Tour route and gentle coastal paths attract riders from across Europe each spring and summer. Walkers are equally rewarded by the GR-92 coastal path, which threads through Tamariu and connects a string of perfect beaches. The sea itself offers snorkelling over Posidonia meadows, diving at pristine rocky reefs, kayaking, paddleboarding and boat hire.

Tamariu Chalet is a lovingly maintained property offering three comfortable en-suite rooms in the main house and a fully self-contained private studio apartment — all sharing access to a tranquil pool, a shaded garden and a patio perfect for long Mediterranean evenings.
